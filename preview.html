01

Product Overview

What is Duolingo?

Duolingo is the world's most downloaded language-learning app (NASDAQ: DUOL). It runs on a freemium model: users can learn for free with ads, or pay for Super Duolingo and Duolingo Max. Its mission is to "develop the best education in the world and make it universally available."

Business Model

  • Subscriptions: Super + Max tiers (~91% of revenue)
  • Advertising: Free-tier users see ads (~$13M/quarter)
  • Duolingo English Test: proficiency certification
  • In-App Purchases: gems, boosts, streak repairs

Why Retention Is Duolingo's Core Strategic Lever

Duolingo grows mainly through the product itself. Much of its acquisition comes from word of mouth and viral social content, so retention becomes the part that keeps growth moving. Users who stay are more likely to become paid subscribers and to bring in new users through referrals.

In the Q1 2024 shareholder letter, CEO Luis von Ahn wrote: "Our DAU-to-MAU ratio, a measure of user engagement, reached a record high thanks to the thousands of product improvements that have compounded over time." Internally, Duolingo uses CURR (Current User Retention Rate) as its North Star metric, which tracks the share of users active today who return 2 weeks later.

02

Problem Statement

Core Problem

Even with DAU growing 51% year over year to 40.5M in Q4 2024, many Duolingo users still leave before the app becomes a daily habit. A DAU/MAU ratio of 34.7% is a record high, but it also means around 65% of monthly users are not coming back every day. For new users, the biggest drop-off happens in the first 10 days. Dormant users who return have Day-30 retention as low as 5%, which leaves a large re-engagement opportunity.

"Duolingo did not win language learning because it had the best content library. It won because it engineered daily practice into a repeatable habit loop, then scaled that loop." Source: Propel, Duolingo Retention Deep-Dive (2025)
