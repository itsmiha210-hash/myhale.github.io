01

Product Overview

What is Duolingo?

Duolingo is a mobile learning app designed to make education engaging and accessible through interactive digital experiences. Best known for its language-learning platform, the app offers courses across 42 languages, alongside subjects such as math, music, and chess. The learning method incorporates gamification to motivate users with points, rewards, and interactive lessons featuring spaced repetition.

Business Model

  • Subscriptions: Super & Max tiers (~91% of revenue)
  • Advertising: Free-tier users see ads (~$13M/quarter)
  • Duolingo English Test: proficiency certification
  • In-App Purchases: gems, boosts, streak repairs

Why Retention Is Duolingo's Core Strategic Lever

Duolingo grows mainly through the product itself. Much of its acquisition comes from word of mouth and viral social content, so retention becomes the part that keeps growth moving. Users who stay are more likely to become paid subscribers and to bring in new users through referrals.

In the Q1 2024 shareholder letter, CEO Luis von Ahn wrote: "Our DAU-to-MAU ratio, a measure of user engagement, reached a record high thanks to the thousands of product improvements that have compounded over time." Internally, Duolingo uses CURR (Current User Retention Rate) as its North Star metric, which tracks the share of users active today who return 2 weeks later.

02

Problem Statement

Core Problem

Even with DAU growing 51% year over year to 40.5M in Q4 2024, many Duolingo users still leave before the app becomes a daily habit. A DAU/MAU ratio of 34.7% is a record high, but it also means around 65% of monthly users are not coming back every day. For new users, the biggest drop-off happens in the first 10 days. Dormant users who return have Day-30 retention as low as 5%, which leaves a large re-engagement opportunity.

"Duolingo did not win language learning because it had the best content library. It won because it engineered daily practice into a repeatable habit loop, then scaled that loop." Source: Propel, Duolingo Retention Deep-Dive (2025)
03

Why This Problem

💸
Revenue at stake

Paid subscribers (9.5M in Q4 2024) mostly come from users who keep using the product. When a user churns before converting, Duolingo loses a potential revenue path. Subscription bookings grew 43% YoY, so this engine is worth protecting.

Source: Duolingo Q4 FY2024 SEC 8-K
📊
DAU/MAU ceiling risk

Duolingo's DAU/MAU went from around 26% in 2020 to 34.7% in Q4 2024. Growth is still strong, but the pace is starting to tighten. Without deeper retention improvements, this ratio could plateau and slow both DAU growth and subscriber conversion.

Source: Duolingo Q4 FY2024 SEC 8-K; StriveCloud Analysis
🌍
EdTech churn is uniquely severe

Education apps historically retain only 1.76% of users after Day 1. Duolingo's 2012 next-day retention was 12%. The company's entire growth story is built on improving this structurally unfavorable baseline.

Source: StriveCloud: Duolingo Gamification Case Study (2025)
04

Clarifying Questions

Q: What's the primary goal?

→ Improve long-term user retention (CURR), especially the gap between first activation and a lasting daily habit. The secondary goal is to convert retained free users to paid.

Q: Which retention stage are we targeting?

→ All three stages matter: (1) New user activation (Days 1–10), (2) Habit maintenance (ongoing), and (3) Re-engagement for dormant users. This case study prioritises Stages 1 and 3 because they offer the clearest return.

Q: What constraints exist?

→ Must not hurt Duolingo's strong notification channel health. Must not compromise learning efficacy (Duolingo's academic reputation matters). New features should be buildable within existing gamification infrastructure.

Q: How do we define success?

→ +5pp improvement in CURR over 12 months, measurable in A/B testing. Leading indicator: Day-30 retention by user segment.

05

User Research & Personas

Based on Duolingo's public data, academic analysis of app usage behaviour, and product teardown research. The segmentation follows Duolingo's user state model: New → Current → At-Risk → Dormant → Resurrected.

🎯
The Ambitious Starter
16–22 · Students

Goals: Learn a new language for personal interests, study abroad opportunities, internships... Starts highly motivated but struggles to stay consistent once academic pressure and social life increase.

Key Frustrations
  • Daily lessons feel difficult to fit into an inconsistent schedule
  • No visible progress milestone to celebrate
  • Missing a streak creates guilt and reduces motivation to return
Day 30 retention: ~15% (Casual segment)
"I was really motivated at first, but after exams and assignments piled up, I slowly stopped opening the app."
🔥
The Streak Keeper
12–20 · Gen Alpha & Gen Z Learners

Goals: Maintain a daily streak as a personal challenge. They care a lot about the habit, so losing a streak feels like a real setback.

Key Frustrations
  • Streak Freeze runs out without warning
  • Losing a long streak is devastating and causes churn
  • Leaderboard competition feels unfair against bots
Day 30 retention: ~40% (Streak Master segment)
"My 200-day streak broke because of a timezone glitch. I almost deleted the app."
🎓
The Serious Learner
28–45 · Professional Learners

Goals: Build real fluency, not only collect XP. They care about lesson depth and speaking practice.

Key Frustrations
  • Free tier lacks conversational speaking features
  • Gamification feels childish for serious study
  • Duolingo Max (AI features) only available in select countries
Highest retention but low conversion to paid without tangible fluency proof
"I want to speak French, not just collect gems. The XP system feels hollow."
😴
The Dormant Returner
12–60 · Any

Goals: Reactivate after a long absence. New Year's resolution or travel trigger sparks return.

Key Frustrations
  • Returning feels overwhelming because they feel too far behind
  • Push notifications become annoying after weeks of silence
  • No personalised returning path
Day 30 retention: ~5% (Inactive segment)
"I came back after 6 months but had no idea where to start."

Retention data sourced from: NextLeap Duolingo Data Analysis; Duolingo Q2 FY2024 SEC 8-K (streak engagement); StriveCloud (2025)

06

Pain Points & User Journey Gaps

1
Streak Loss = Permanent Churn
Critical

A broken streak, especially after 30+ days, is one of the clearest churn triggers. Users can feel punished instead of supported. Days 2–10 are the highest-risk window.

Affected: Streak Keepers, Ambitious Starters · Data: Streak Masters have 40% Day-30 retention vs 15% for Casual Learners
2
Weak Re-onboarding for Returners
High

Dormant users who come back do not get a personalised re-entry path. The app shows the same homepage as before, which can make months of missed content feel overwhelming.

Affected: Dormant Returners · Data: Inactive users: only 5% Day-30 retention
3
Notification Fatigue
High

Duolingo's aggressive push notifications are famous. But at scale, once opt-out occurs, a key retention lever is permanently lost.

Affected: All segments, especially Dormant Returners · Data: Notification opt-outs remove one of Duolingo's main retention channels
4
Gamification Ceiling for Serious Learners
Medium

The core streak + XP loop retains casual users effectively but fails to satisfy learners seeking genuine fluency. They plateau and churn once they feel they're 'just gaming'.

Affected: Serious Learners · Data: Duolingo's DAU/MAU ratio hit 34.7% in Q4 2024, which is strong, but the ceiling may be close
5
Low Social / Accountability Hooks
Medium

Friend Streaks and leaderboards exist but friend discovery is friction-heavy. Social accountability is one of the strongest drivers of habit formation but remains underutilised.

Affected: Ambitious Starters, Streak Keepers · Data: Only 1/3 of DAUs use Friend Streak despite it being a key retention feature
07

Pain Point Prioritisation

Using a 2×2 Impact vs. Addressability framework, the priority should be problems that are high-impact and that Duolingo can directly influence through product changes.

High Impact / High Addressability
→ Streak Loss = Churn
→ Weak Re-onboarding
High Impact / Lower Addressability
→ Notification Fatigue (channel health)
Lower Impact / High Addressability
→ Social Hooks (Friend Streak expansion)
Lower Impact / Lower Addressability
→ Gamification ceiling for Serious Learners

Focus: Streak Loss and Re-onboarding are the highest-ROI problems. Notification Fatigue should be treated as a guardrail problem. It is important to protect, but harder to fully solve.

08

Solutions

Prioritised with the RICE framework (Reach × Impact × Confidence ÷ Effort). All scores use a 1–10 scale.

Replace the binary streak-freeze model with a tiered Streak Shield system. If a user with a 7+ day streak misses one day, they automatically receive a grace-period repair option. For streaks longer than 30 days, offer a 'Comeback Repair' within 48 hours.

Why this works: Streak loss is the #1 churn trigger. One improvement to the streak mechanic previously drove +6% DAU in Duolingo's internal experiments, according to Jorge Mazal, Head of Product.

When a user returns after 14+ days of inactivity, show a personalised 'Welcome Back' flow. This includes a 3-question micro-quiz to re-diagnose their level, a short refresh lesson for missed content, and a new 7-day re-engagement challenge with visible rewards.

Why this works: Dormant returners have only 5% Day-30 retention. A more guided re-entry flow can reduce the feeling of being overwhelmed. Duolingo already saw a 20% next-day retention lift from moving sign-up after the first lesson, and the same 'show value first' logic applies here.

Build an ML model that personalises notification timing, frequency, and copy for each user based on open rates, session times, and engagement patterns. Add a 'Notification Fatigue Score' as a guardrail, so the system reduces frequency for users who are likely to opt out.

Why this works: Duolingo's notification channel is one of its strongest retention levers, but opt-out removes a user from it. Protecting this channel helps retention compound over time.

Launch 'Study Squads', small groups of 3–5 users with shared weekly XP goals. Squads can set a goal, celebrate milestones together, and create light peer accountability. This can build on the existing Friend Streak infrastructure.

Why this works: Friend Streak is used by only 1/3 of DAUs despite being proven to improve retention. Group accountability can be a much stronger motivator than solo streaks, especially when users feel seen by a small group.

Introduce a 'Fluency Passport', a visible and shareable certificate of language progress tied to completed story-mode arcs, conversation practice sessions, and vocabulary milestones. This positions Max subscription as the path to credentialed fluency.

Why this works: Serious Learners can become high-LTV users, but they leave when progress does not feel useful in real life. Tangible, shareable milestones can support both retention and Max conversion.

Risks & Mitigations

Streak Shield abuse
Medium

Users may exploit the grace-period mechanic to keep streaks without real engagement.

Mitigation: Require at least 1 lesson completed in the 48-hour repair window. Track XP earned, not just login.
Re-onboarding feels condescending
Medium

Returning advanced users may feel infantilised by a placement quiz.

Mitigation: Make re-onboarding skippable with one tap. Frame it as an 'optional warm-up', not a required step.
Notification personalisation increases cost
Low

An ML-based notification system needs meaningful engineering and infrastructure investment.

Mitigation: Phase 1: use rule-based segmentation with 3 tiers: high, medium, and low engager. Phase 2: move to an ML model.
Study Squads cannibalize solo retention loops
Low

Group dynamics may frustrate users whose friends are inactive, causing additional churn.

Mitigation: Match Squads by activity level using similar DAU patterns. Allow a silent solo mode inside a squad.
